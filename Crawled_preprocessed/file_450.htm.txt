brokers will provide the ability to write covered calls and to use married puts when you open your options account this article will focus on the function of married puts in a portfolio or as part of a trading strategy married puts refer to the combination of two different purchases one of a stock position and one of a put option let s consider the following purchase let s say you choose to buy shares of xyz for per share and one xyz september put for shares x with this combination you have purchased a stock position with a cost of share but have also bought a form of insurance to protect yourself in case the stock declines below before the expiration third friday in september you should remember that for a put to be considered married the put and the stock must be bought on the same day and you must instruct your broker that the stock you have just purchased will be delivered if the put is exercised does a married put make sense when you actually think about it why would anyone ever choose to employ this particular strategy some of you might think that it s almost like buying a stock and knowing that it will fall to pieces that is an interesting argument but a married put should be looked at as a form of insurance rather than as an expectation of a certain outcome in essence you are expecting an appreciating stock price but you are also preparing yourself in case your stock takes a steep plunge to learn more about making money on falling stock see prices plunging buy a put obviously you can t purchase put protection for every stock in your portfolio because such costs would significantly eat into your portfolio s returns over time you can however use a married put strategy in certain circumstances particularly if you are unsure about a certain stock how many times have you bought a stock only to slap your forehead in disgust when reading a quote a few days later with a married put your downside is protected an idea that is quite appealing in today s market a married put is a lot like an insurance policy on a home you purchase your home the stock but also insurance the put in case the home is damaged the stock price plummets in his course options for beginners luke downey leverages simple analogies like this to demystify options which are often unjustly thought of as overly complicated or advanced for the typical investor practice makes perfect consider the following example today is january and you purchase shares of abc at share and two february puts for per contract you have also told your broker that you intend for this to be a married put transaction so let s consider whether this position makes for a good trade in any transaction you have to gauge your exposure or your risk in the given example you have a total cash outlay of excluding commissions you have paid for the stock and for your insurance policy if the stock goes up above you are now in a profit position on paper and won t have to consider the put option because it will expire worthless if however abc plummets to a share then the value of the insurance quickly becomes apparent if you were faced with a stock value of at the february expiry you would be looking at a loss of x contrast this with the loss you would have incurred without the married put strategy x as you work through the example it becomes obvious that a married put makes a lot more sense for an investment in which you expect an event to raise the price significantly which if left undelivered will result in a dramatic fall such a situation might be an upcoming earnings release date when the company will either deliver positive cash flow or lose its credibility completely in such a case a married put can protect your downside while allowing you to participate fully in any upside unlike a covered call which limits your potential gains for more on covered call strategies check out covered call strategies for a falling market things to consider before you tie the knot if you are new to options your broker might limit your exposure to puts by confining you to married put positions because two investments are tied together you will be sure to have shares available if the put is exercised furthermore unlike a covered call a married put does not increase the value of your portfolio over time because you are buying the put option you are incurring a cost for the price of this protection any costs whether they are trading fees insurance commissions or management expenses are all factors that lower your return this is an important point to remember when considering put options for your portfolio conclusion married puts are one of the few ways that a new investor can protect the downside of an investment this protection comes at a cost and it is important to use this strategy only in certain situations if you think that a particular stock will go up but you are wary of a possible disaster then the married put strategy could be quite useful married puts can provide a great means of limiting your losses and helping you learn the usefulness of puts in a portfolio as in any options trade always make sure that you consider the worst case
